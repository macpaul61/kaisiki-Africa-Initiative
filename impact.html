Where It All Began

Kaisiki African Initiative was born in Tanzania — a direct response to a simple but urgent reality: too many bright, capable girls were being left behind. Whether due to poverty, distance from school, or deep-rooted social barriers, their path to education was being cut short before it could truly begin.

We are not a large international organisation spreading thin across a continent. We are a focused, committed team working closely alongside students, families, teachers, and community leaders in Tanzania to create genuine, lasting change from within — one girl at a time.

This page reflects our honest, on-the-ground impact. Where we are today. And where we are headed.

Amina's Second Chance

Secondary Student · Scholarship Recipient

Amina was in Form 2 when her father lost his job and her school fees went unpaid. She was sent home three times in one term. Through Kaisiki's scholarship program, her fees were covered and she was able to sit her exams. She passed. She is now in Form 4 and wants to study nursing.

👩🏾
📍 Dodoma Region
"

Neema Finds Her Voice

Mentorship Program · Peer Leader

Neema grew up believing school was "for boys." After joining one of our community mentorship sessions, she began to see herself differently. She convinced her parents to let her return to school, recruited two friends to join alongside her, and is now a peer mentor — helping other girls in her village do the same.

👩🏾‍💻
📍 Mwanza Region
"

Baraka's Digital Dream

Skills Program · Digital Literacy

Baraka had never touched a computer before joining our digital literacy workshop. Within three months she was helping her mother manage orders for their small tailoring business using a simple spreadsheet. "I thought technology was for rich people," she said. "Now I know it is for anyone willing to learn."

Africa is the Vision

Tanzania is where we plant our roots — deep, careful, and committed. But the need for girls' education and community empowerment extends far beyond our borders. As our foundation grows stronger, we aspire to carry what we've built here to other countries across Africa, one community partnership at a time.
